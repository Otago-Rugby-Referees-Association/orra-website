Training and Development Resources

ORRA is committed to the continuous development of our referees. This page provides access to training materials, development programs, and resources to help you excel in your officiating career.

Referee Development Pathway

ORRA follows the New Zealand Rugby referee development pathway, providing structured progression from grassroots to elite level officiating.

Development Levels

  • Entry Level: Introduction to refereeing, basic laws and positioning
  • Club Level: Senior club rugby officiating
  • Provincial Level: Representative and higher-grade matches
  • National Level: Pathway to NPC and higher honors

Training Programs

Pre-Season Training

Comprehensive preparation for the upcoming season including:

  • Law updates and clarifications
  • Fitness testing and conditioning
  • Skills workshops and scenario training
  • Communication and conflict resolution

In-Season Development

Ongoing development opportunities throughout the season:

  • Monthly training sessions
  • Video analysis and review
  • One-on-one coaching and mentoring
  • Peer learning and discussion groups

Fitness Requirements

Physical fitness is essential for effective refereeing. All ORRA referees must meet the following standards:

  • Complete annual fitness testing
  • Maintain conditioning throughout the season
  • Follow NZ Rugby fitness guidelines for your level

Mentoring Program

ORRA offers a mentoring program matching experienced referees with those developing their skills. Benefits include:

  • Personalized feedback and guidance
  • Match day support and observation
  • Career pathway advice
  • Access to experienced referee knowledge

Coaching and Assessment

Regular coaching sessions and performance assessments help track your progress:

  • Video analysis of your performances
  • Written feedback reports
  • Development plans and goal setting
  • Access to referee coaches at matches
