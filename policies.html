ORRA Policies

ORRA operates under a framework of policies designed to ensure fair, professional, and effective management of rugby officiating in Otago. All members are expected to familiarize themselves with these policies.

Code of Conduct

Core Principles

All ORRA referees are expected to:

  • Uphold the integrity and spirit of rugby
  • Apply the laws fairly and consistently
  • Act with professionalism at all times
  • Respect players, coaches, and fellow officials
  • Maintain physical fitness appropriate to their level
  • Continue professional development
  • Avoid conflicts of interest

Appointment Policy

Referee appointments are made by the Appointments Officer based on:

  • Referee availability and stated preferences
  • Appropriate grade matching for skill level
  • Development and progression opportunities
  • Fair distribution of matches among referees
  • Avoidance of conflicts of interest
  • Recent performance and fitness

Conflict of Interest Policy

Referees must not officiate matches where a conflict of interest exists, including:

  • Current or recent membership of participating clubs
  • Close family members playing or coaching
  • Financial interests in match outcomes
  • Personal relationships that could affect impartiality

Referees must declare any potential conflicts to the Appointments Officer.

Fitness and Attendance Policy

Fitness Requirements

  • All referees must complete annual fitness testing
  • Maintain fitness throughout the season
  • Meet NZ Rugby standards for their appointed level
  • Notify development officer of any fitness concerns

Match Attendance

  • Confirm match appointments promptly
  • Arrive at venue at least 45 minutes before kickoff
  • Notify appointments officer immediately if unable to attend
  • Repeated late withdrawals may affect future appointments

Incident Reporting Policy

Referees must report the following incidents within 48 hours:

  • Any red card or citing
  • Serious foul play not resulting in a card
  • Misconduct by players, coaches, or spectators
  • Injuries requiring medical attention
  • Match abandonment or significant delays
  • Any incidents affecting match integrity

Uniform and Equipment Standards

All referees must maintain professional appearance standards:

  • Official ORRA or NZ Rugby referee uniform
  • Clean and well-maintained kit
  • Appropriate footwear for conditions
  • Required equipment (whistle, cards, coin, watch)
  • Communication devices if provided

Privacy and Confidentiality

ORRA respects the privacy of all members. Personal information is:

  • Collected only for legitimate association purposes
  • Stored securely and accessed by authorized personnel only
  • Not shared with third parties without consent
  • Maintained in accordance with the Privacy Act

Complaints and Disputes

Complaint Procedure

Complaints should be submitted in writing to the ORRA Secretary and will be:

  • Acknowledged within 5 working days
  • Investigated fairly and impartially
  • Resolved in accordance with natural justice principles
  • Kept confidential to parties involved

Social Media Policy

When representing ORRA or discussing rugby officiating online:

  • Maintain professional standards
  • Avoid commenting on specific matches or decisions
  • Respect confidentiality of association matters
  • Do not bring the association into disrepute
  • Remember posts are public and permanent

Financial Policies

ORRA maintains transparent financial management:

  • Annual budget approved by committee
  • Regular financial reporting to members
  • Reimbursements processed within 14 days
  • Annual accounts audited and presented at AGM

Policy Updates

Policies are reviewed annually and updated as needed. Members will be notified of any significant changes. For questions about any policy, contact the ORRA Secretary.
