Standards & Results

ORRA referees must meet specific fitness and law knowledge standards appropriate to their level. These standards ensure referees are physically capable and have the required knowledge to officiate safely and effectively.

The Bronco test is a shuttle run fitness assessment that measures cardiovascular endurance and recovery. All referees must complete this test annually.

About the Bronco Test

The Bronco test consists of five 20-meter and five 40-meter shuttles (total 300m). It tests both speed and endurance recovery - essential for refereeing rugby matches.

Testing Schedule: Pre-season (usually February/March) and mid-season retest available.

üìö CRRDF Law Test Standards

Law test assessments ensure referees have current knowledge of rugby laws and their application. Tests are conducted online through the official platform.

Level Pass Standard Frequency Requirements
Level 5 Excellence: 80%+ Every year Must achieve minimum excellence pass annually
Level 4 Merit: 75%+ Every second year Must achieve minimum merit pass biennially
Level 3 Achieved: 70%+ Every year Must achieve minimum achieved pass annually
Level 2 Working toward 70%+ Every year Should be working toward 70% or better annually
Level 1 Optional As desired Complete Associate Referee Course. Online law test optional

Law Test Information

Format: Online multiple-choice assessment covering all aspects of rugby laws

Duration: Typically 60-90 minutes

Content: Based on current World Rugby laws and interpretations

Preparation: Study materials and practice tests available through World Rugby and ORRA resources

Not Meeting Standards?

If you're struggling to meet the required standards, don't worry - support is available:

  • Fitness Support: Personal training plans, group sessions, and guidance available
  • Law Study: One-on-one support, study groups, and additional resources provided
  • Development Plans: Work with Coaching & Development officer to create achievable goals

Contact the Coaching & Development officer for confidential support.

Standard Updates

Standards are reviewed annually and may be updated to reflect changes in CRRDF (Community Rugby Referee Development Framework) guidelines or World Rugby requirements. Any changes will be communicated well in advance.
